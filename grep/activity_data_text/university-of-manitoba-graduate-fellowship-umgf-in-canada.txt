Eligibility Criteria :
Are you planning to pursue a postgraduate or doctoral degree at the University of Manitoba? If you are, then make sure you go through the eligibility criteria to ensure you meet all of the University of Manitoba graduate fellowship requirements.
Eligibility Criteria for the University of Manitoba Graduate Fellowship (UMGF):
The graduate fellowship is open to students of all nationalities.
A minimum GPA of 3.0 is required for consideration for this award. However, some departments may require a higher GPA for eligibility.
Applicants must enroll full-time in a research-based master’s or PhD program at the University of Manitoba’s Faculty of Graduate Studies.
Students pursuing programs without a research/thesis, such as Master of Business Administration (MBA), Master of Finance (MFin), and Master of Supply Chain Management (MSCM), are not eligible for the UMGF.
Applicants must demonstrate exceptional potential as researchers and graduate students, highlighting their promise in both roles.
Recipients of the award must maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0 and avoid receiving grades lower than C+ during their scholarship tenure.
The UMGF cannot be combined with any other scholarship of equal or greater monetary value.
Eligibility criteria are standardized, but individual faculties are responsible for selecting recipients from their respective programs.

Benefits :
If you meet the eligibility criteria above, do not forget to read the various benefits of this graduate fellowship in Canada that Opportunities Circle has compiled for you below.
Benefits of the University of Manitoba Scholarship in Canada:
UMGF provides substantial financial assistance to recipients, covering tuition fees, living expenses, and research-related costs.
Master’s level awards are valued at up to $14,000 per year, which can be received for either 12 or 24 months, for a maximum total of $28,000.
Doctoral-level awards can reach up to $18,000 annually for 12, 24, 36, or 48 months, with a maximum total value of $72,000.
The 2024-2025 UMGF is, hence, offered for various durations, allowing students to tailor the award to their academic plans and research timelines.
UMGF encourages and supports research activities, giving applicants ample time to conduct in-depth research and contribute to their field of study.
University of Manitoba graduate fellowship is awarded based on academic merit, which acknowledges and rewards the recipients’ exceptional academic achievements. It serves as recognition of their scholarly potential and dedication.
Canada is renowned for its world-class educational institutions. UMGF recipients can access first-class academic programs and renowned faculty members by studying in Canada.

Application Process:
How to Apply for the University of Manitoba Graduate Fellowship 2024-2025?
To apply for this fellowship, visit the official website of the University of Manitoba.
Confirm that the program you intend to pursue at the University of Manitoba qualifies for the UMGF.
Apply for admission to your desired master’s or doctoral program at the University of Manitoba. Fellowship applications are typically considered alongside your program application.
Students must submit all UMGF applications to their respective academic departments.
The respective department recommends students to the Faculty of Graduate Studies after selecting them.
If applicable, provide proof of English language proficiency through standardized tests like IELTS or TOEFL, as the university requires.
Application Deadline for the University of Manitoba Graduate Fellowship in Canada:
The deadline to apply for the University of Manitoba graduate fellowships varies depending on the department.
