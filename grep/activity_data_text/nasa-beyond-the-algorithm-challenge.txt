Eligibility Criteria :
If you are looking for top international competitions, you must apply for NASA’s Beyond the Algorithm Challenge. However, before you do, go through the eligibility criteria below to ensure you meet all the requirements of this challenge.
Eligibility Criteria for NASA Beyond the Algorithm Challenge 2025:
The challenge is open to individuals, teams and organizations that are legally recognized under U.S. law and are in good standing.
Individuals must be U.S. citizens or permanent residents and be at least 18 years old.
Organizations must be incorporated in and have their main place of business within the United States.
Similarly, teams should consist of qualified individuals or organizations and must be led by a U.S. citizen or permanent resident who is at least 18 years old.
U.S. government employees may participate only if doing so outside the scope of their official roles and must not use any privileged access.
Foreign nationals may only participate if they work for an eligible U.S. entity and reside in the United States.
All registered participants are accountable for the compliance of their team members, employees, subcontractors and officers with the challenge rules.

Benefits :
Do you meet the eligibility criteria above? If yes, you would not want to miss this competition by NASA. Keep on reading to know more about the benefits of this Beyond the Algorithm Challenge. Opportunities Circle has listed the benefits below.
Benefits of NASA Beyond the Algorithm Challenge:
The NASA Beyond the Algorithm Challenge offers various benefits to participants. It is a paid competition where participants will win cash prizes.
Top three winning solutions will receive $100,000 each.
Participants gain skills that help protect and support communities affected by flooding.
Similarly, this challenge offers a unique opportunity to explore flood analysis through various fields of study.
Participants will have the chance to work alongside government agencies like NASA, non-profit organizations and private entities.
By participating in this competition, applicants will be able to apply their skills in new contexts.

Application Process:
Want to know how to apply for the NASA Beyond the Algorithm Challenge? Click the ‘apply now’ button below to go to the official portal.
Before you apply, ensure you meet the criteria and requirements of this challenge.
Applicants should start by registering for the program and then follow the submission process.
The competition has three phases, each with its own deadline and requirements.
As part of phase 1, applicants will have to prepare a five-page White Paper and a two to three minute video.
Similarly, the finalist selection will take place in August 2025. Up to ten finalists will be selected for the pitch event.
Make sure to apply before the deadline.
