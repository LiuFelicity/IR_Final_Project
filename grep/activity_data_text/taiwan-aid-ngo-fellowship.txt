Eligibility Criteria :
Do you want to become a Taiwan AID fellow? If yes, you must apply for this international fellowship program. However, before you do, make sure to go through the eligibility criteria and requirements for the 2025 NGO Fellowship in Taiwan.
Eligibility Criteria for Taiwan AID NGO Fellowship 2025:
To be eligible for the Taiwan AID NGO Fellowship Program, candidates must be between 25 to 40 years old.
Fellows should be NGO professionals who are ready to engage with the program’s activities and contribute meaningfully to the fellowship cohort.
Moreover, the 2025 NGO Fellowship Program welcomes individuals from various professional backgrounds within the NGO sector. This includes areas such as community development, environment, education, gender equality, human rights advocacy, media and information technology, LGBTQ+, medical services, and social welfare.
Exceptional applicants with expertise in other relevant fields of NGO work can also be considered.
Candidates must have a minimum of three years of relevant work experience within the NGO sector to be eligible for the Taiwan AID fellowships.
Moreover, applicants should demonstrate professional excellence, self-motivation, and the ability to work independently.
Eligible candidates for the Taiwan fellowship 2025 must hold citizenship from countries within specific regions, including Southeast Asia, South Asia and Northeast Asia.
Proficiency in both written and spoken English is a prerequisite for all applicants. Similarly, fluency in Mandarin will be advantageous.

Benefits :
Do you meet the eligibility criteria above? If you do, you would not want to miss this opportunity in Taiwan. Read the various benefits of the Taiwan AID NGO Fellowship 2025 below.
Benefits of Taiwan Aid NGO Fellowship Program 2025:
Fellows will gain hands-on experience and practical insights into the operations of Taiwanese NGOs.
All applicants will participate in various workshops, roundtable events, future project collaborations and other activities like event preparation, fundraising and project management.
Moreover, the Taiwan AID NGO Fellowship Program is a fully funded fellowship.
It will include a three-week placement period, where fellows will be welcomed by their host NGOs and will actively participate in day-to-day operations, gaining experience of the inner workings of Taiwanese NGOs.
In addition, Taiwan AID will cover fellows’ expenses including airfare, lodging (shared rooms), meals, and transportation. Taiwan AID fellows will get per diem during the three-week NGO placement.
The 2025 NGO Fellowship is an excellent opportunity to live and work in Taiwan.

Documents Required for 2025 NGO Fellowship Program:
Curriculum vitae/CV (1 page on a letter-size paper)
Personal statement (1 page)
Personal headshot
Recommendation letters (2)

Application Process:
The application process for the fully funded Taiwan Fellowship Program 2025 is online.
How to apply for the Taiwan NGO Fellowship Program 2025?
To apply for the Taiwan AID Fellowship program, applicants must click on the ‘apply now’ button below or visit the official application portal.
Before applying, ensure you meet the eligibility criteria and requirements.
Fellows must carefully fill in the 2025 NGO Fellowship program application form and provide all the necessary documents, as incomplete applications will not be eligible.
Moreover, the entire application process for the 2025 Taiwan Fellowship is online.
Interested applicants must submit the application form and required documents before the deadline if they wish to participate in this fellowship in Asia.
